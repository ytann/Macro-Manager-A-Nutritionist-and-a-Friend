PMOS & Metabolic Syndrome Nutrition

Standard caloric deficits fail. Macro composition dictates hormonal response. 40/35/25 split vs Standard Diet. Naked carbs vs Buffered carbs.

Macro Splits: Standard vs PMOS

Standard diet = high carbohydrate (55%). PMOS optimal = low glycemic, high protein, moderate fat (40C/35P/25F). High protein → satiety ↑, insulin ↓.

Naked vs Buffered Carbs

Naked carbs = carbohydrates consumed in isolation. Buffered = carbs + protein/fat. Buffer → gastric emptying ↓ → glucose spike ↓.

Postprandial Glucose Response

Blood glucose trajectory post-ingestion. Naked carbs → steep hyper-glycemic peak. Buffered → stabilized, flat curve. Stable glucose = stable insulin.

Insulin AUC (Area Under Curve)

Total insulin secreted. PMOS = hyperinsulinemia. Lower AUC = improved androgen profile. Buffering carbs reduces insulin AUC significantly.
