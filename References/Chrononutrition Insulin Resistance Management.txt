Chrononutrition & Circadian Insulin Sensitivity: Metabolic Dysfunction Management
Endogenous Circadian Physiology & Diurnal Glucose Clearance Mechanisms
Glucose metabolism regulation relies SCN central pacemaker + peripheral clocks.1 Diurnal variations dictate glucose tolerance.2 Tolerance highest morning, lowest evening/night.2 Endogenous circadian system overrides behavioral cycle impacting evening glucose tolerance reduction.3 Circadian misalignment (simulated night work, shift work) ↓ glucose tolerance independent behavioral cycle.1
SCN-HPA Axis & Hepatic Glucose Production (HGP) Dynamics
SCN regulates HGP diurnal variation.5 SCN → PVN → CRH release.5 PVN → anterior pituitary corticotrophs → ACTH release.5 ACTH → adrenal gland → glucocorticoid (cortisol) release.5 SNS co-regulates glucocorticoid rhythms.5 Circadian HGP changes correlate blood FFA + cortisol levels.4 Nighttime HGP ↑ → insulin sensitivity ↓.4 Daytime HGP ↓ → insulin sensitivity ↑.4 NIDDM patients exhibit rhythmic nighttime IR ↑ independent blood glucose, insulin clearance, exercise, food intake, sleep.4 Biological evening = reduced β-cell responsiveness, reduced early-phase insulin secretion.2 Obesity/T2D patients exhibit attenuated/phase-delayed circadian metabolic rhythms.2
BMAL1, CLOCK, & Peripheral Insulin Sensitivity Pathways
Feeding behavior = dominant zeitgeber synchronizing peripheral clocks.1 Central pacemaker aligns metabolic tissue.1 Molecular pathways dictate peripheral sensitivity.
Pancreatic & Gut Clocks: GLP-1 synthesized intestinal L-cells + pancreatic α-cells.6 GLP-1 stimulates insulin secretion via GCK (Hexokinase 4).6 CLOCK + BMAL1 bind E-box promoter → circadian GCK expression control.6 GCK acts glucose sensor.6 GCK affinity low (  ), lacks glucose-6-phosphate inhibition, phosphorylates glucose across broad range (3-15 mM).6 BMAL1 disruption = abnormal GCK function, glucose metabolism dysregulation.6 Dietary signals regulate GCK via insulin-mediated SREBP1c pathway.6 Postprandial insulin → SREBP1c activation → GCK transcription ↑.6 GCK activity ↑ feeding, ↓ fasting.6 GCK activators (dorzagliatin) align liver physiological rhythm pre-meal.6
Muscle, Adipose, & Renal Clocks: BMAL1 → HK2 → Glut4 pathway regulates peripheral insulin sensitivity (muscle/adipose).6 HK2 regulates renal glucose reabsorption + glycogen deposition.6
Melatonin-Induced Insulin Inhibition & MTNR1B Genotype
Endogenous melatonin circadian rhythm impairs biological night glucose homeostasis.7 Late food intake aligns melatonin spike.7
Melatonin Signaling Mechanisms: Melatonin → MT2 receptor binding.8 MT2 activation → cAMP ↓ → PKA ↓.8 Outcome = glucose-stimulated insulin secretion ↓.8 IP3 pathway transiently compensates nighttime IR, ↑ insulin secretion initially.9 Prolonged melatonin → cAMP pathway dominance → insulin secretion ↓.9 Decreasing blood glucose concentration subsequently ↓ stimulatory effect, normalizing insulin.9
MTNR1B Risk Variant (G-allele): MTNR1B rs10830963 G-allele = elevated melatonin, early sleep timing.10 G-allele carriers upregulate MT2 receptors.8 Risk variant + late food intake = extreme cAMP inhibition, massive glucose-stimulated insulin secretion ↓.7 Reduced insulin release exacerbated circulating melatonin.8 Genotype differences manifest β-cell function reduction.7

Adipocyte Insulin Resistance & Lipolysis
Sedentary subjects develop skeletal muscle + subcutaneous fat cell IR.39 204 sedentary vs 336 physically active subjects analyzed.39 Insulin inhibits triglyceride hydrolysis (antilipolysis), stimulates triglyceride formation (lipogenesis).39
   * Responsiveness: Maximum insulin effect for antilipolysis (60% inhibition) + lipogenesis (twofold stimulation) = similar active vs sedentary.39
   * Sensitivity: Half-maximal effective concentration ↓ 10-fold sedentary subjects (  ).39 Antilipolysis sensitivity highly associated physical activity independent BMI, age, sex, waist-hip ratio, fat-cell size.39
   * Receptor Expression: mRNA expression insulin receptor, IRS-1, IRS-2 ↓ sedentary subjects.39
   * FFA Dynamics: Hyperinsulinemia suppresses FFA, however FFA remains significantly higher sedentary vs active women (  ).39 Elevated FFA causes lipotoxicity, impairs peripheral insulin signaling.41
Breaking Sedentary Time & MVPA Timing
Interrupting prolonged daytime sitting ↓ plasma glucose AUC 31.5% (95% CI -49.3 to -13.8), ↓ insulin AUC 26.6% (95% CI -39.6 to -9.9).42 No significant triglyceride AUC change.42 Breaking sedentary time ↓ liver fat, ↓ IR, prevents T2D.40
MVPA Timing Optimization: Diurnal variations influence exercise capacity.40 Afternoon/evening MVPA ↓ IR up to 25% vs even daily MVPA distribution.40 Morning MVPA = no IR advantage.40 Evening PA directly counteracts natural biological evening glucose tolerance deficit.40 Evening PA limits cortisol-induced evening IR.43

         1.          2.          3.
